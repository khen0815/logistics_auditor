## What We Actually Sell

Henderson Performance is a South African logistics margin audit service for e-commerce and parcel-heavy SMEs. The offer is not generic logistics consulting and not enterprise SaaS. It takes messy courier exports/invoices and turns them into a line-by-line recovery and prevention plan.

Core value:

- Find courier overcharges and billed-weight jumps.
- Catch volumetric billing and package-size leakage.
- Identify SKUs/orders where free delivery is destroying margin.
- Produce dispute-ready waybill evidence for courier/account-manager queries.
- Recommend practical packaging rules, SKU actions and monthly monitoring.

The strongest first offer is a low-friction audit for founder-led SMEs: a Mini Leak Check, Starter Audit, or one-month courier leak diagnostic before trying to sell a retainer.

## Updated Target Market

The first prospect list should **not** lead with Superbalist, Bash/TFG, Woolworths, Mr Price, Takealot-scale retailers or cross-border distributors. Those are slower procurement targets.

Best-fit early prospects are:

- South African founder-led e-commerce brands.
- Roughly 80-600 domestic parcels/month, with stretch targets above 1,000/month.
- Shopify, WooCommerce, Bob Go, uAfrica, The Courier Guy, Pudo, Fastway, Aramex, Skynet or similar courier/export signals.
- Domestic South African dispatch profile: “nationwide delivery”, “free delivery over R___”, local courier language, returns, tracking.
- Mixed SKUs or packaging complexity: clothing, activewear, skincare, supplements, gifts, candles, homeware, pet products, coffee, speciality food.
- Reachable decision makers: founder, owner, operations manager, e-commerce manager, fulfilment/warehouse lead, bookkeeper or finance manager.

## Domestic Dispatch Filter

Exclude or heavily down-rank prospects likely to have a significant ratio of international dispatches. The current tool, proof, messaging and courier assumptions are strongest for local South African courier-margin leakage.

Avoid first-batch prospects if they emphasize:

- International shipping as a core sales channel.
- Export orders, import/export logistics, SADC/cross-border freight, customs or freight forwarding.
- Tourism/export gifting aimed mainly at overseas buyers.
- Wine export clubs or export-heavy DTC channels.
- B2B import/export distribution rather than local parcel courier delivery.

Wine, specialty food and gifting can still fit, but only where the online store appears mainly domestic/nationwide South African delivery.

## Qualification Questions

Use these before asking for files:

1. Roughly how many domestic South African parcels do you ship per month?
2. What percentage of your orders are international or cross-border? If significant, do not prioritize for this list.
3. Which courier/platform do you use most often: Bob Go, The Courier Guy, Pudo, Aramex, Skynet, Fastway, uAfrica, direct courier account, other?
4. Can you export one month of courier data with waybill, order ID, actual weight, billed/charged weight, dimensions and cost?
5. Do you offer free delivery above a threshold?
6. Do packers choose packaging manually, or do you have fixed packaging rules by product/SKU?
7. Who owns courier-cost control internally: founder, operations, fulfilment, finance, bookkeeper or e-commerce manager?
8. Would a once-off Mini Leak Check or Starter Audit be easier than a monthly commitment?
